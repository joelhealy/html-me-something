What are Goodeids?

Goodeids are a family of fish (Goodeidae) consisting of approximately 40 species. The vast majority of goodeids are members of a sub-family named Goodeinae. These fish are livebearers and are endemic to Central Mexico. There are a few species in a second sub-family named Empetrichthynae that are egg-layers and are endemic to the Southwest United States. The current theory assumes that these fish all descended from a common ancestor and that the two sub-families were isolated by volcanic and seismic activity over a million years ago and developed separately.
